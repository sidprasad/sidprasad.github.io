Work

Tufts University

My Honors Thesis on a Stepwise Interactive Debugger for Bison was supervised by Prof. Kathleen Fisher . I also designed PBug, a parser-generator language under Prof Fisher's guidance as part of an independent study. My Senior Capstone explored distributed value and cloud based caching mechanisms for proxies. Sponsored by Prof. Fahad Dogar, a technical report on the project can be found here . I also built a networked based interpreter system for Scheme under Prof. Dogar's supervision.


INRIA

In the summer of 2014, I worked at INRIA Saclay with the Parsifal Team and developed a Rewrite Verification System. A technical report for this system can be found here.

I.I.T. Delhi

In the summer of 2013, I worked on image restoration problems dealt with in Bertalmio and Sapiro's paper 'Image Inpainting' under the project 'Acquisition, Representation, Processing and Display of Heritage Sites' at the Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi, where I developed a basic inpainting algorithm.

Bio + Stuff I Do

I grew up in New Delhi, India and recently graduated from Tufts University. I'm about to start work as a Software Engineer at Microsoft in Redmond, WA.

I play the guitar and love jazz. Until very recently, I played for the Tufts University Small Jazz Ensemble. I also (infrequently) write a blog on my thoughts on jazz-based musicology and theory.

I played tennis as a kid, and watch a lot of sports. I'm a huge Arsenal fan, and (almost) never miss a match.
